Verbatim definition (from the World Bank's metadata for the
SI.POV.GINI indicator, copied 2026-04):

  "Gini index measures the extent to which the distribution of
   income (or, in some cases, consumption expenditure) among
   individuals or households within an economy deviates from a
   perfectly equal distribution. … Thus a Gini index of 0
   represents perfect equality, while an index of 100 implies
   perfect inequality."

  (Note: the World Bank reports the index on a 0–100 scale, while
   academic and Statistics-Canada usage typically reports the
   coefficient on a 0–1 scale. The two are equivalent — divide by
   100 to convert.)

Why we cite this:
  Our Gini computation in math/concentration.py applies this same
  inequality coefficient to the distribution of contract dollars
  across vendors within a procurement category — instead of
  household income, we measure dollar concentration among suppliers.
  We report on the 0–1 scale (Statistics-Canada convention).
