Source: U.S. Department of Justice, Antitrust Division
        Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (official explainer page)
URL:    https://www.justice.gov/atr/herfindahl-hirschman-index

Verbatim quote (from the page's body text, copied 2026-04):

  "The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) is a commonly accepted measure
   of market concentration. It is calculated by squaring the market share
   of each firm competing in the market and then summing the resulting
   numbers …

   The agencies generally consider markets in which the HHI is between
   1,500 and 2,500 points to be moderately concentrated, and consider
   markets in which the HHI is in excess of 2,500 points to be highly
   concentrated."

Why we cite this:
  Our HHI computation in math/concentration.py uses these exact bands —
  <1,500 competitive · 1,500–2,500 moderate · >2,500 highly concentrated —
  to interpret category-level vendor concentration in Canadian government
  procurement. The DOJ page is the canonical, freely-accessible source
  for the formula and the threshold convention.
